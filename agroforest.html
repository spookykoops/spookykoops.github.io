The Food Chains:


FOOD CHAIN 1: Sunlight > icelandic blue Grass > Cow > Shiitake Mushroom
FOOD CHAIN 2: Sunlight > Flower(s) > Bee > Ant
FOOD CHAIN 3: Sunlight > Wheat/Barley > Pig > Wolf
FOOD CHAIN 4: Sunlight > icelandic blue Grass > Rabbit > human
FOOD CHAIN 5: Sunlight > icelandic blue Grass > Ants > Beetle > Spider

How would these work together in a food web?


SUNLIGHT: Used to spur photosynthesis for grass, flowers, wheat/barley, and others.
ICELANDIC BLUE GRASS: Used as a food source by herbivores such as cows and pigs. Like everything in this ecosystem, once it dies, it will be used as a food source by decomposers like the shiitake mushroom.
BEE: Used to pollinate plants and flowers, as well as a food source by ants and spiders.
ANT: Engaged in a predator-prey relationship with bees, other small insects, and other plants. Also utilized as a food source by carnivorous arachnids and beetles.
WOLF: Feeds on pigs, rabbits, and other herbivores.
PIG: Used as a food source by wolves and humans. Feeds on grasses, wheat, barley, and flowers.
RABBIT: Feeds on grasses, flowers, wheat, and barley. Is also used as a food source by humans and other carnivores/omnivores.
BEETLE: Uses ants as a food source. Also is preyed upon by spiders, and occasionally mammals such as rabbits or pigs.
SPIDER: Preys upon ants and beetles. May be used as a food source by mammals such as pigs.
WHEAT/BARLEY: May be used as a food source by pigs, cows, and humans.
