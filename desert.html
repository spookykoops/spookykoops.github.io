The Food Chains:


FOOD CHAIN 1: Sunlight > century plant > desert bighorn sheep > bobcat > bacteria
FOOD CHAIN 2: sunlight > desert willow > Desert Bighorn sheep >horsefly > sonoran desert toad > kangaroo rat > coyote
FOOD CHAIN 3: sunlight > chain fruit cholla > Cactus Ferruginous Pygmy Owl > Ferruginous Hawk
FOOD CHAIN 4: sunlight > desert orchid > honey bee > black widow >spider wasp > elf owl
FOOD CHAIN 5: sunlight > palo verde tree > blister beetle > hairy scorpion > tarantula > ants > thorny devil

How would these work together in a food web?


HAIRY SCORPION: Feeds off of blister beetles, and is preyed upon by tarantulas and other such carnivores, such as the owls within the ecosystem.
SPIDER WASP: Preys on black widows and other arachnids. Is then used as a food source by elf owls and other such creatures like the cactus ferruginous pygmy owl.
BLACK WIDOW: Feeds on honey bees and other insects, such as blister beetles. Is the preyed upon by spider wasps and other such creatures.
ELF OWL: Preys upon spider wasps and other such insects. May then be eaten by bobcats or the ferruginous hawk.
BOBCAT: Eventually, like other creatures in this food chain, is used as a food source by bacteria and other decomposers. Preys upon desert bighorn sheep and other herbivores.
HORSEFLIES: Used as a food source by the sonoran desert toad, and other creatures such as the cactus ferruginous pygmy owl.
HUMANS: While not included in the food chains, humans will certainly be present in the desert,
