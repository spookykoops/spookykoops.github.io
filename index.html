WHAT IS BIOSPHERE 3?

Buildings are burning.

Species become extinct every day.

The human race is nearly decimated.

As a result, a group for scientist looked to the heavens, searching for an alternative to their suffering. Seeing Mars as a suitable alternative to earth, they set about preparing for the evacuation of the entire human race, to a closed environment within the final frontier. Enter biosphere three. Mankind's salvation will be a towering structure about 1,500 miles across, 1.5 miles tall, 1,500 miles lengthwise, and constructed on Mars. As a result, it will be completely airtight, and rely solely on the oxygen provided by the organisms. The biosphere will be separated into multiple parts, each one devoted to a certain type of ecosystem. Of course, before we can fully begin to move in the organisms required, we will have to do a little “prepping”. First, we will move the plants and smaller organisms into the biosphere, in order to begin the many cycles required for life, using small amounts of CO2, nitrogen, and water released into the many environments. Once the cycles are sufficiently established, larger and larger organisms will be moved into the ecosystem, until it is fit for humanoids to survive within.

CONSTRUCTION:

The Biosphere will be composed of glass, steel, and aerogel (an insulating material which can be used as an alternative to concrete).

As mentioned above, it will be separated into multiple domes, each one composed of steel-reinforced glass, with no tint, in order to maximize the amount of sunlight that gets through.

Multiple access tunnels and tubes will be spread throughout the biosphere, in order to allow quick and easy transport between biosphere sections.

The biosphere will also be slightly pressurized, in order to mimic the gravity on earth.

Any cargo intended for the biosphere during construction will be extensively screened, to prevent any unwanted species from arriving.

LIMITING FACTORS:

We will include exactly one predator for every 2 prey.

We would make sure that each predator relies on several varied food sources, in order to prevent the balance from being upset, if one species or another goes extinct.

We will use the biosphere’s limited size to keep a species’ numbers in check, by tracking each species's population limit, and ensuring that they remain within that limit.

We will make sure to include competition for various food sources, in order to create competition between the species.

We will make sure not to interfere if a parasite or disease becomes too widespread, in order to allow the weak species to be filetered out, as they would in a real ecosystem. (unless it is created by human interference).

We will try to mimic the different seasons of each section of the biosphere, by varying the temperature, and other such factors, in order to create the limiting factor of seasons.
