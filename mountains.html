The Food Chains:


FOOD CHAIN 1: Sunlight > common Juniper > Elk > Mountain Lion
FOOD CHAIN 2: Sunlight > Boulder Raspberry > Moose > black Bear
FOOD CHAIN 3: Sunlight > algae > tadpole shrimp > minnows > salmon > pike > grizzly bear
FOOD CHAIN 4: sunlight > douglas fir > Mule Deer > wolf > slippery jack mushrooms > bighorn sheep > coyote
FOOD CHAIN 5: sunlight > algae > fairy shrimp > minnow > cutthroat trout > pike > pike > Human

How would these work together in a food web?


MOOSE: Will eat plants, and help move the carbon and nitrogen throughout their respective cycles. Will also be used as a food source by black and grizzly bears, as well as coyotes. Once it dies, it may be used as food by the fish (if it dies near a river) or decomposers such as slippery jack mushrooms.
SLIPPERY JACK MUSHROOMS: Help speed up the decaying process on many dead creatures. Are in turn used as a food source by herbivores such as a moose or chipmunk.
COYOTE: As a carnivore near the top of the food chain, it wonâ€™t have many predators, other than the occasional bear that may attack it. As it is with many of the creatures on this food chain, it will be decomposed by decomposers, and may be scavenged by creatures such as a wolf.
ALGAE: Used as a food source by smaller fish such as the fairy shrimp. Also is used to decompose dead aquatic creatures, and occasionally a mammal which gets to close to the water and dies.
CUTTHROAT TROUT: Used as a food source for larger predators such as pike or bears. oNce it dies, may be decomposed by algae or, occasionally, slippery jack mushrooms. Also is a predator on smaller fish such as minnows.
DOUGLAS FIR: Can be used as a food source for herbivores such as bighorn sheep. Uses photosynthesis to provide oxygen for other species in the biosphere. Once it dies, it will decompose, and become a food source for decomposers such as slippery Jack mushrooms.
COYOTES & WOLVES: Predators that prey on herbivores such as bighorn sheep. May also be eaten by a grizzly bear, if they threaten it. Like everything else in this biosphere, once it ies,it becomes food for decomposers.
HUMAN: May be consumed by a large carnivore like a bear. For the most part, will be a prey species on fish, sheep, etc.
