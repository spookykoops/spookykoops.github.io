The Food Chains:


FOOD CHAIN 1: Phytoplankton > Plankton > Shrimp > Fish > Humans
FOOD CHAIN 2: Fish > Seal > Shark
FOOD CHAIN 3: Plankton > Krill > Turtle >Great white shark
FOOD CHAIN 4: Plankton > Krill > Fish > Squid>Human
FOOD CHAIN 5: Plankton > Krill > Jellyfish > Sea Turtle

How would these work together in a food web?


PHYTOPLANKTON: Used as a food source for plankton and other such small species. Also helps carry out photosynthesis, keeping the whole ecosystem in check.
PLANKTON: Preys on phytoplankton, and is used as a food source for shrimp, krill, and other smaller species of fish. Like all of the other species in this biosphere, plankton will eventually die and be used as food by decomposers.
KRILL: Used as a food source y fish, turtles, and small jellyfish. Also is a predator that preys on plankton.
TURTLE: Preys on Krill and jellyfish. Also is used as a food source by sharks, and other large aquatic species.
SQUID: Preys off of Krill and fish. May also be used as a food source by humans, sharks, etc.
HUMANS: Prey off of fish. Mya be used as a food source by sharks, but only extremely rarely.
FISH (tuna, whitefin, etc.): Used as a food source by sharks, squid, and humans. May prey off of krill, other fish, or maybe even plankton depending on the size of the fish in question.
