The Food Chains:


FOOD CHAIN 1: Banana Plant (primary producer)> sloth (primary consumer)> Jaguar (secondary consumer)

FOOD CHAIN 2: Sunlight > Orchid> Butterfly > Spider> Snake

FOOD CHAIN 3: Plankton/Algae> water flea> minnow > altum angel > catfish >human

FOOD CHAIN 4: sunlight > ceiba tree > Caterpillar> Bird> Snake>Bengal Tiger

FOOD CHAIN 5: sunlight >Banana> Monkey> Tiger

How would these work together in a food web?


Sunlight: Sunlight provides energy for producers within the rainforest.

Bird: Birds are omnivorous creatures that consume insects and seeds. They are also the food supply for many different predators.

Tiger: Tigers are a predator that is at the top of the food chain in the rainforest. This means that there is nothing that hunts them and they have to die naturally. However, their remains may be decomposed, or eaten by alligators, and other such animals which are perfectly content with eating a dead body.

Ceiba Tree: Trees are an important species that can convert carbon dioxide to oxygen in large amounts. This allows for many animals to be able to breathe. They also will act as a food supply for creatures such as caterpillar, and other herbivores.

Sunlight: Used to fuel a plant’s photosynthesis

Human: May use animals such as catfish as a food source.

Banana: Fruit, such as the banana, will be used as a food source for herbivores, humans, and other omnivores.

Caterpillar: Insects, such as caterpillars, will be used as a food source for creatures such as birds and spiders/snakes, as well as a “predator” in a sense, that preys on plants.

Minnows: Minnows, and other fish, will be sued as a food source by larger aquatic, and sometimes land-based species, in addition to eating off algae and plankton in the water.
